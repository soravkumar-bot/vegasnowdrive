Electric Vehicle Guide

EV Basics for Beginners

Everything you need to know before switching to electric — from charging to range anxiety.

🔋
Understanding Range
EV range varies by speed, temperature, and load. In cold weather, expect up to 20% reduction. Always plan charging stops with a 15–20% buffer.
⚡
Charging Levels Explained
Level 1 (home socket): 3–5 mph. Level 2 (home charger): 15–30 mph. DC Fast Charge: 150–350 mph. Use DC fast charging for road trips only.
📍
Route Planning with Charging
Use apps like PlugShare or your car's native routing. Always have a backup charging option mapped. Plan for 20–30 min stops every 150–200 miles.
🌡️
Battery Care in Heat & Cold
Precondition your battery before driving. Park in shade when possible in heat. In cold climates, keep the car plugged in overnight to preserve range.
💰
Total Cost of Ownership
EVs cost more upfront but save on fuel and maintenance. No oil changes, fewer brake jobs (thanks to regen braking), and cheaper per-mile energy costs.
🔌
Home Charging Setup
Install a Level 2 EVSE (J1772) for best results. Most owners charge overnight. A dedicated 240V circuit covers 95% of daily driving needs with ease.
Car Rental Guide

Smart Rental Tips

Avoid hidden costs and get the most out of every rental with these expert insights.

💳 Cost
Avoiding Hidden Fees
Always read the fine print. Watch out for airport surcharges, fuel top-up fees, and damage waiver upsells you may not need.
🚗 Selection
Picking the Right Car
Match the car to your route. Mountain roads call for AWD. Long highways benefit from fuel-efficient sedans. City trips need compact cars.
🛡️ Insurance
Rental Insurance Explained
Your personal auto policy or credit card may already cover rentals. Check before paying for CDW. Know the difference between LDW and SLI.
Driving Safety

Stay Safe on Every Drive

Safety is the foundation of every great road trip. Follow these essentials before and during your journey.

👀
Defensive Driving
Maintain a 3-second following distance. Scan ahead 12–15 seconds, not just the car in front. Anticipate hazards before they become emergencies.
😴
Fatigue Management
Stop every 2 hours or 100 miles. Signs of fatigue include blinking frequently, drifting lanes, or missing exits. No destination is worth a drowsy drive.
🌧️
Wet Weather Driving
Slow down 5–10 mph in rain. Avoid cruise control on wet roads. Hydroplaning happens at speeds as low as 35 mph on standing water.
